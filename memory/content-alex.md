### Quest 3.1

**Label:** Core Quest 3.1 · The Quiz

**Title:** Fourteen Steps

**Surface:** [apartmentlist.com/quiz](https://www.apartmentlist.com/quiz) → [apartmentlist.com/matches](https://www.apartmentlist.com/matches)

**Action steps:**
- Take the quiz from the start as Alex. Answer every step as them: budget, location, laundry, commute, everything.
- Pay close attention to the budget and commute steps. Alex has specific numbers. Note whether the quiz can handle that level of specificity.
- Notice the steps that feel off or that Alex would pause on.

**Reflection:** Where did the quiz let Alex be specific, and where did it flatten their search into something that doesn't fit the spreadsheet?

### Quest 3.2

**Label:** Core Quest 3.2 · Sign Up & Matches

**Title:** What Comes Back

**Surface:** [apartmentlist.com/signup](https://www.apartmentlist.com/signup) → [apartmentlist.com/matches](https://www.apartmentlist.com/matches)

**Heads-up:** Use a test account — format `youremail+test[date]@apartmentlist.com`

**Action steps:**
- Finish signing up as Alex (with a +test account) and land in their matches.
- Take in the first impression: how many matches, how relevant do they look, which neighborhoods are surfacing.
- Would Alex trust this list, or reach for the other tabs?

**Reflection:** After signing up, what did Alex's matches look like? Did the quiz pay off here, or does the list feel like it missed something?

### Quest 4.2

**Title:** Reading the Fine Print

**Reflection:** What did the listing tell Alex, and what's still unanswered? Where does the LDP stop short of what Alex needs to feel confident about a place?

**Title:** The First Message

**Surface:** Any shortlisted listing's contact form on [apartmentlist.com](https://www.apartmentlist.com)

**Action steps:**

- Write a message as Alex. Ask the questions they would actually ask: smart home features, exact monthly cost, earliest available move-in.
- Notice what Apartment List sets up around the message: suggested questions, expected response time, anything that tells Alex what happens next.

**Reflection:** How did it feel for Alex to send the first message? Did Apartment List prepare them for what comes next, or did they hit send and wonder if anyone was on the other end?

### Quest 5.2

**Label:** Core Quest 5.2 · Tour Scheduling

**Title:** Lining Up a Look

**Surface:** Tour scheduling UI on any shortlisted listing on [apartmentlist.com](https://www.apartmentlist.com)

**Action steps:**

**Reflection:** How did booking a tour feel for Alex? Did Apartment List make the process easier or harder?

### Quest 5.3

**Title:** Through the Screen

**Surface:** Virtual tour or live remote tour on any shortlisted listing on [apartmentlist.com](https://www.apartmentlist.com)

**Action steps:**

**Reflection:** What did the virtual walkthrough let Alex see, and what stayed out of frame? Could they almost commit from this, or does the kitchen still need to be seen in person?

### Quest 6.1

**Title:** The Decision

**Surface:** Alex's shortlist on [apartmentlist.com/shortlist](https://www.apartmentlist.com/shortlist)

**Action steps:**
- Open Alex's shortlist, or picture the two places they've been going back and forth on.

- Pick one. Which place is Alex signing for? Notice the deciding factor.

**Reflection:** Which place did Alex pick, and what made it the one? Was Apartment List part of how they got there, or did the deciding factor come from somewhere else?

---

### Quest 6.2

**Label:** Core Quest 6.2 · A Scenario

**Title:** The Lease Arrives

**Scenario:**
The lease arrived. There's an early termination clause: leaving before 12 months costs two months' rent. There's also a parking add-on that wasn't in the listed price — $175 a month. Alex has been budgeting $4,200. This lease puts them at $4,375 before negotiation. The leasing office picks up the phone tomorrow morning. The signed lease is due in 72 hours.

**Sit with it:**
- How does Alex feel about a monthly fee that wasn't in the quoted price?
- How does the early termination clause land for someone who planned this move 90 days ahead?
- What does Alex do with 72 hours and a leasing office that picks up the phone?

**Reflection:** What does Alex do with the 72 hours? Sign, push back, ask about the parking? And what does this moment say about where Apartment List's job ends and the property's begins?

---

### Quest 6.3

**Label:** Core Quest 6.3 · The Renter Hub Walk

**Title:** Walking the Renter Hub

**Surface:** [apartmentlist.com/renter-hub](https://www.apartmentlist.com/renter-hub)

**Action steps:**
- Open Renter Hub. Walk through all five sections: Split rent payments, Deposit alternatives, Renters insurance, Moving help, Internet & utilities.
- Read the descriptions, ratings, and pricing as Alex. Picture what their first month in the new place looks like with each.
- Mark what would actually help Alex, and what wouldn't apply.

**Reflection:** Of the five Renter Hub sections, which ones would Alex actually use, and which would they skip? Where does Renter Hub feel like the right service at the right moment, and where does it feel built for a different kind of renter?
