This is dogfooding.

Using the product the way our renters do. The goal is empathy, not quality assurance. The point is to close the gap between what we think the renter experience is and what it actually is.

How the journey works

1

2

Walk seven chapters

From the the search to the signing, all the way to the welcome mat. Each chapter has its own surfaces and its own quests.

3

Real product actions on apartmentlist.com. Logged out, logged in. The quiz, the matches, the LDPs, the Renter Hub. Every surface a renter touches.

4

Collect Field Notes

Copyable takeaways you can drop into Slack to share with other A-Listers. Your observations are the data.

Level Upper

Alex the Ambitious

Climbing the ladder, one square foot at a time.

"I've earned this. Show me the upgrade."
What they're looking for▶
Must-haves
  • 1BR with a door that closes
  • Real kitchen with counter space
  • 12-min commute to the office
  • Rent under $4,200/mo
Wants
  • 2BR if Hayes Valley fits
  • Bike storage (not a wall mount)
  • Building gym worth using
About Alex▶

Alex is a senior engineer in San Francisco. Three years in the same studio apartment, their salary has gone up but the square footage hasn't. The bike lives on the wall because there's nowhere else to put it. The lease non-renewal came last week, and Alex is treating it as permission to finally upgrade.

The wishlist is specific: a real kitchen with counter space, a bedroom door that closes, somewhere to put the bike that isn't a wall mount. SoMa is the safe pick because the commute would be twelve minutes. Hayes Valley keeps coming back if the budget can stretch.

Alex hunts with a spreadsheet (the spreadsheet was drafted before the lease notice came). Three platforms run in browser tabs at once. The standards aren't flexible, but the budget might be.

Achiever

Jordan the Rising Star

A new city, a new job, and Max along for the ride.

"Three real options. One that works for Max. Before Monday."
What they're looking for▶
Must-haves
  • Pet-friendly (Max actually welcome)
  • Move-in within 18 days
  • Replies within 24 hours
  • Rent under $2,400/mo
Wants
  • A yard for Max
  • Park within walking distance
  • Real home office
  • Short hybrid-day commute
About Jordan▶

Jordan got the offer last week. New job in Seattle, three weeks to land. Right now they're standing in their Portland apartment looking at a corner that's about to fill up with moving boxes, with Max the golden retriever asleep on the dog bed that has to get packed first. Excited about the role, but slightly underslept and a little bit panicking.

The shortlist has shape but not edges. A yard would be ideal, a park within walking distance will do. A real home office matters because the new role is hybrid and the kitchen table can't keep doing double duty. Pet-friendly isn't a perk for Jordan. Max needs to actually be welcome.

Eighteen days isn't a lot of time to chase ghost listings or wait three days for a property to respond. Jordan wants the map view first. They'll come back to the quiz later, maybe.

City Hopper

Taylor & Riley

Two pairs of eyes, one new city to explore.

"We'll know the place when we both feel it. A street, a sunset, a coffee shop on the corner."
What they're looking for▶
Must-haves
  • Walkable neighborhood
  • Real local food scene
  • A street they hear from the window
  • Combined rent under $2,500
Wants
  • Two-bedroom unit
  • Natural light Riley can shoot in
  • Photography scene outside galleries
  • Tours bookable without a phone call
About Taylor & Riley▶

Taylor and Riley have been in Chicago for three years, the longest they've stayed anywhere. Polaroids from Lisbon are still on the fridge, a zine from Mexico City lives on the counter, and Riley's camera lives next to the toaster. The plan, which has been forming for months, is to leave Chicago for Austin.

Riley wants walkable streets and a photography scene that lives outside galleries. Taylor wants incredible food. They both want a neighborhood they can hear from the window. The unit matters less than what's around it.

They search together. Listings get screenshot-shared between phones with one-line captions like "this one" or "no please." Riley reads Reddit threads about Austin neighborhoods. Taylor texts friends who've lived there. Different approaches, same couch, mutual veto. You play as Taylor. Riley is a narrative character throughout the journey.

Pathfinder

Jamie the Resilient Parent

Every choice has to work for both of us.

"The numbers, the school, the safety. No surprises."
What they're looking for▶
Must-haves
  • 2BR (Emma's room is non-negotiable)
  • Strong, verifiable school district
  • Safe streets and a walkable park
  • Move-in before kindergarten starts
  • Rent under $3,200/mo
Wants
  • Pet-friendly (Emma keeps asking)
  • Transit to family support network
  • In-unit laundry
  • A buzzing local park
About Jamie▶

Jamie is a single parent in suburban Philadelphia, but the family support system is in Jersey. Their daughter Emma is starting kindergarten in five months, which means whatever happens next has to happen fast. The plan is Jersey City. The schools are good, the transit is doable, but the budget is tight, and Jamie hasn't fully committed yet because committing means it's real.

School quality is the first filter, then safe streets and a walkable park. A two-bedroom is non-negotiable. Pet-friendly stays on the list because Emma keeps asking about a small dog.

Jamie reads every review and cross-references school ratings on three sites. They've joined three NJ parent Facebook groups under their work email and lurk in all of them. They want one good place. Not perfect, just good. Emma is a narrative character throughout the journey.
