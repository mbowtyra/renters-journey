Welcome to theRenter's Journey

An A-Lister's invitation to walk in our renters' shoes.

Scroll to begin ↓

This is dogfooding.

Using the product the way our renters do. The goal is empathy, not quality assurance. The point is to close the gap between what we think the renter experience is and what it actually is.

How the journey works

1

Choose your renter

Four personas. Pick whose path you want to walk.

2

Walk seven chapters

From the the search to the signing, all the way to the welcome mat. Each chapter has its own surfaces and its own quests.

3

Complete dogfooding quests

Real product actions on apartmentlist.com. Logged out, logged in. The quiz, the matches, the LDPs, the Renter Hub. Every surface a renter touches.

4

Collect Field Notes

Copyable takeaways you can drop into Slack to share with other A-Listers. Your observations are the data.

Put yourself in your renter's shoes and pay attention.

About Taylor & Riley▶

Taylor and Riley have been in Chicago for three years, the longest they've stayed anywhere. Polaroids from Lisbon are still on the fridge, a zine from Mexico City lives on the counter, and Riley's camera lives next to the toaster. The plan, which has been forming for months, is to leave Chicago for Austin.

Riley wants walkable streets and a photography scene that lives outside galleries. Taylor wants incredible food. They both want a neighborhood they can hear from the window. The unit matters less than what's around it.

They search together. Listings get screenshot-shared between phones with one-line captions like "this one" or "no please." Riley reads Reddit threads about Austin neighborhoods. Taylor texts friends who've lived there. Different approaches, same couch, mutual veto. You play as Taylor. Riley is a narrative character throughout the journey.

▸ Where Jordan is tonight

Jordan got a new job, and they're up late journaling and thinking about the move. Here's what was running through their head.

Jordan's journal · Tuesday, late

The job offer just came in! Excited for the new role, but they said I have to start in three weeks, so I'll have to start looking for a place. I'll need a second room or space for an office, it sounds like.

Texted Courtney, she lives in Seattle. She said Capitol Hill might be a vibe, but also said it's gotten expensive. Browsed a couple Seattle subreddits, but got overwhelmed by people venting about cost. I still have Craigslist on my phone since the last time I moved, but it doesn't seem like Seattle uses Craigslist the same way.

Max is asleep in the corner on his dog bed. How will Max feel about all this? I'll have to make sure he really likes the new place: a backyard would be best, but a park within walking distance would do. I'll have to rent a car. Are dogs allowed in U-Hauls? I'm not sure. I feel so overwhelmed!

About Jamie

Jamie is a single parent in suburban Philadelphia, but the family support system is in Jersey. Their daughter Emma is starting kindergarten in five months, which means whatever happens next has to happen fast. The plan is Jersey City. The schools are good, the transit is doable, but the budget is tight, and the moment the lease arrives is the moment it all becomes real.

School quality is the first filter, then safe streets and a walkable park. A two-bedroom is non-negotiable. Pet-friendly stays on the list because Emma keeps asking about a small dog.

Jamie reads every review, cross-references school ratings, and lurks in three NJ parent Facebook groups. They want one good place. Not perfect, just good. Emma is a narrative character throughout the journey.
